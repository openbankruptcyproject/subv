Subchapter V of Chapter 11

Subchapter V is the small-business-only stream of Chapter 11 reorganization, created by the Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019 and effective February 19, 2020. This research project tracks Sub V doctrine, eligibility math, plan structure, and empirical filing patterns.

What Subchapter V does

Subchapter V applies a streamlined Chapter 11 process to small-business debtors. Key features:

  • No creditors' committee by default (unless the U.S. Trustee orders one)
  • Sub V trustee appointed in every case (a hybrid role — oversees, doesn't run the business)
  • 90-day plan deadline from the petition date
  • No absolute priority rule in cramdown — equity owners can retain interests if the plan pays creditors fairly and in good faith
  • Discharge at plan completion for individual debtors; at confirmation for entity debtors

The eligibility math

Sub V eligibility turns on the debtor's aggregate noncontingent liquidated debt. The threshold has changed over time:

  • February 2020 (effective date): $2,725,625 (from the original 2019 statute, COLA-adjusted)
  • March 2020 (CARES Act): raised to $7,500,000 to address COVID-era small-business needs
  • June 2024: CARES Act sunset; threshold reverts to original (currently $3,024,725 with COLA adjustment)

Cases above the current ceiling fall into standard Chapter 11, which is significantly more expensive and procedurally demanding.
