Subchapter V Practitioner Traps

Common pitfalls in Sub V practice. This is for general educational reference; check current local rules and recent case law for any specific case.

The 90-day plan deadline

Section 1189 requires the debtor to file a plan within 90 days of the order for relief. Extensions are available only on a finding of "circumstances for which the debtor should not justly be held accountable." Courts apply this standard strictly. Missing the deadline can be grounds for conversion or dismissal.

Sub V trustee fees

Sub V trustees are paid as a separate professional under § 330 from the estate. Trustee fees can run substantial in complex cases. Plan budgets that don't account for trustee fees adequately are subject to confirmation objection.

The "engaged in commercial or business activities" boundary

If the business has wound down before filing, the debtor may not satisfy § 101(51D)'s "engaged in" requirement. Some courts apply this test as of the petition date (debtor must be currently operating); others apply it more liberally (debtor was engaged in commercial activity that produced the debt). Local circuit law controls.

Affiliate aggregation

Section 101(51D)(B)(ii) excludes corporations whose debts when combined with affiliates' debts exceed the ceiling. Watch for:

  • Holding company structures with multiple subsidiaries
  • Co-owned LLCs sharing debt obligations
  • Franchise relationships where the debtor is a franchisee with affiliate-related debt

Single-asset real estate exclusion

Single-asset real estate debtors (per § 101(51B)) are explicitly excluded from Sub V eligibility under § 101(51D)(B)(iii). Mixed-use cases that have some operational business activity in addition to real estate may still qualify, but the operational activity must be substantial.

Plan modification mechanics

Sub V plans can be modified post-confirmation under § 1193(b). Modifications require:

  • Court approval after notice and hearing
  • Compliance with § 1191's confirmation requirements
  • For consensual plans: maintain at least the original recovery rates per impaired class

Income tax treatment

Discharge of debt in Sub V is generally not taxable under IRS Code § 108(a)(1)(A). But the rule has technical limits, and timing of the discharge (confirmation vs. plan completion) can affect tax-year recognition. Coordinate with tax counsel.

Conversion to standard Chapter 11

If the debtor's debts grow post-petition (e.g., affiliates pull in more debt) above the Sub V ceiling, the case can be converted to standard Chapter 11. Conversion is non-trivial because:

  • The Sub V trustee role ends
  • A creditors' committee may be formed
  • Standard absolute priority applies in cramdown
  • Costs increase substantially
